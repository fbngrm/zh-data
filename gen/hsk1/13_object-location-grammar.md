## Header
grammar 1: 的 字短语 (de zì duăngũ)
## Explanation
A 的 phrase is composed of an adjective, pronoun or verb followed by 的; the subsequent noun is often omitted if it's clear what 的 refers to.
## Syntax

## Note

## Examples

桌子上有两杯茶哪一杯是你的? 那杯大的是我的<br />
Zhuīzi shàng you liăng bãi chá, nă yì bēi shì ni de? Nà bei dà de shì wo de5.<br />
There are two cups of tea on the table, which cup is yours? That big cup is mine.<br />

我去买一点儿吃的。<br />

## Header
grammar 1: 看 和 看见 (kàn hé kànjiàn)
## Explanation
If the verb kàn is followed by jiàn, the result of kàn is emphasized.
## Syntax

## Note

## Examples

昨天我看见丽丽了<br />
Zuótiān wo kanjiàn Lili le<br />
I saw Lily yesterday.<br />

你看见我的杯子了吗?你看,你的杯子在桌子上。<br />
Ni kànjiàn wo de bēizi le ma? Nĩ kàn, ni de bēizi zài zhuōzi shàng.<br />
Have you seen my cup? Look, your cup is on the table!<br />
