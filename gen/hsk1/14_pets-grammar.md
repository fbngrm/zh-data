## Header
grammar 1: 形容词谓语句 (xíngróng cí wèi yũ jù)
## Explanation
In Chinese, adjectives can be used directly as predicate in sentences; the adverb hěn, (very) is often added before the adjective. To negate an adjective as predicate sentence, we can add 不 before the adjective.
## Syntax
subject + 很 (or) 不 + adjective
## Note

## Examples

你的小猫很漂亮 。<br />
Nĭ de xião mão hěn piàoliang .<br />
Your cat is very beautiful.<br />

我们 学校的老师不多。<br />
Women xuéxiào de lăoshỉ bù duō .<br />
There are not many teachers in our school.<br />

## Header
grammar 1: 多 (duō)
## Explanation
When we use 多 as adjective it refers to many or a high quantity. Its antonym is 少(little/few).
## Syntax

## Note

## Examples

我有很多朋友 。<br />
Wõ you hěn duīpéngyšu .<br />
I have a lot of friends.<br />
